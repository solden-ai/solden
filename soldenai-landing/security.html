Security & Trust

Defaults an audit committee can sign off on.

Least-privilege scopes. Deterministic decision rules. Append-only audit at the schema layer. A vendor footprint we can defend in a DPIA.

Compliance posture

SOC 2 controls are implemented across access control, change management, audit logging, encryption, and incident response. The formal SOC 2 Type 1 audit is scheduled; the Type 1 report will be available under NDA on completion, with the Type 2 observation window starting immediately after. GDPR compliant by design: EU launch market, EU data residency available, and a DPA executed with every customer before production data flows.

We do not store cardholder data and we are not a payment processor. Solden never debits, credits, or initiates a vendor payment. Treasury runs the payment batch out of your ERP on its own schedule.

Controls

Encryption at rest

AES-256 for all stored data. Per-tenant Fernet envelope keys for sensitive material like OAuth tokens.

Encryption in transit

TLS 1.2+ end-to-end. HSTS preloaded; sub-processor connections all enforce TLS.

Append-only audit

Every record event lands in an audit table protected by database triggers, not application logic. Tamper-evident by design.

Tenant isolation

Row-level filtering on every query plus per-tenant credential boundaries. Cross-tenant access is impossible at the schema layer.

Least-privilege scopes

Gmail/Outlook: read + label only. We do not request gmail.send for vendor outreach (Solden authors zero vendor email).

Deterministic decisions

Match tolerance, sanctions checks, budget gates, 3-way validation, and routing are rules. The model reads input and writes prose, no vote on outcomes.

Secret management

Production secrets injected from the platform vault; no plaintext in environment files. Required-secret guard crashes prod startup if any secret is missing.

Backup & recovery

Postgres point-in-time recovery (7-day window). Restore is rehearsed on every release candidate.

Access control

SSO via OIDC. SCIM provisioning available on enterprise plans. Role-based authorisation enforced in the API layer.

What data Solden touches

In: invoice PDFs, invoice email bodies and attachments, PO and GRN extracts from your ERP, vendor master extracts (read-only), bank-detail records (encrypted at rest, surfaced only for the validation gate).

Out: AP record writes back to your ERP (bills, GL postings, vendor balance updates) on approval; Slack, Teams, NetSuite SuiteApp, and SAP Fiori cards for human decisions; structured audit events.

Never out: emails to your vendors. Solden does not author vendor body text and does not send vendor-facing email. Vendor outreach stays with the operator.

Data Processing Agreement

We sign a DPA with every customer before production data flows. The standard agreement is GDPR-aligned and includes the EU Standard Contractual Clauses where relevant. Request a copy.

Sub-processors

The infrastructure footprint is intentionally small. Each sub-processor processes data for a defined purpose and is bound by a signed DPA with us, or accessed via an OAuth scope the customer grants and can revoke at any time.

"Customer-controlled" means the data lives in the customer's existing tenant on that vendor's platform; Solden authenticates via OAuth scopes the customer grants and can revoke. We do not store the underlying data outside our own AWS environment.

Incident response

Customers are notified within 72 hours of any confirmed security incident affecting their data, faster where the incident is material. The runbook is tested quarterly. Post-incident reports are shared with affected customers and include scope, root cause, mitigations, and forward-looking changes.

Responsible disclosure

Researchers can email security@soldenai.com with vulnerability reports. We acknowledge within one business day and aim to remediate critical issues within 7 days. We do not pursue good-faith researchers.
