Why now

Fragmentation grows with agents.

Back-office tool stacks keep growing. Most of the next additions will be agents.

An agent that does work is just another participant in a workflow that already lives across too many surfaces. Without a runtime, every agent is its own island, with its own state, and the coordination cost compounds.

Solden gives the agents somewhere to stand. The same record that holds human work holds agent work. The same audit chain that proves what a person did proves what an agent proposed. The same ownership trail that names who decided names which agent recommended and which human signed off.

The fragmentation isn’t a phase. It’s the new shape of work. The runtime is how you hold it.

The shape of the problem

Every back-office workflow lives in a patchwork of tools and one person’s head.

A finance team closing the month runs on a patchwork of tools. The vendor invoice in Gmail. The PO match in NetSuite. The approval routed in Slack. The accrual in a spreadsheet that lives on someone’s desktop. The audit binder in a shared drive.

The tools each do their job. The work between them lives nowhere. When the person who held it leaves, or forgets, or goes on vacation, the work goes with them.

That’s not an automation problem. That’s a coordination problem. And it’s getting worse, not better, as more of the work moves to agents that also need somewhere to stand.

How Solden works

One record per workflow.
Five steps. Every move signed.

Every back-office workflow becomes a record. The record holds its own state, its own owner, what it’s waiting on, what broke, its history, and the policy that decided each call. It lives across your tools without belonging to any one of them. Your tools render it. Solden holds it.

  1. 01

    Captured

    Invoices arrive in your inbox, your ERP queue, a vendor portal, or a PEPPOL UBL feed. Solden reads the unstructured input. The model lifts vendor, amount, dates, references, and line items onto a typed record. The record exists the moment work enters the building.

  2. 02

    Validated

    The cross-checks your ERP has data for but doesn’t run. Three-way match against PO and GRN. Sanctions screen. Duplicate scan. Budget tolerance. Each gate is rule-based, versioned, and replayable. Rules decide. The model only writes the explanation.

  3. 03

    Decided

    When a record needs a human call, Solden surfaces it where the approver already works. Gmail sidebar, Slack card, Teams card, NetSuite SuiteApp panel, SAP Fiori extension. Same record, rendered where the approver is. The decision lands back on the record. Audit captures who clicked, why, and the policy version that authorized it.

  4. 04

    Posted

    Approval lands. Solden writes to your ERP — NetSuite, SAP, QuickBooks, Xero — with retry, idempotency, and a bounded override window. The record knows what posted, when, and by which policy. Treasury runs the payment from the ERP on its own schedule. Solden authors zero vendor email.

  5. 05

    Sealed

    The chain closes when the bill is paid and the bank matched. Every transition is signed with sha256. Each event hashes the event before it. Strip a row and the chain breaks. Six months later your audit committee reconstructs the entire workflow from one file. The proof writes itself as a side effect of doing the work.
