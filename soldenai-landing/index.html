How it works

From inbox to ERP, with one record across every step.

Solden does the cross-checks the ERP doesn't run. It never replaces your ERP, never authors vendor master, never triggers payment. Treasury still owns the payment batch.

1

Capture

Invoices land in Gmail, Outlook, an ERP queue, or PEPPOL. Solden lifts the line items, totals, taxes and references — structured input goes straight through, unstructured runs through extraction with vision fallback.

PDF · UBL · email body
2

Validate

3-way match against PO and GRN. Vendor master and bank-detail check. Duplicate, sanctions and budget gates. Match-tolerance is a deterministic rule, not a model guess.

PO ↔ GRN ↔ Invoice
3

Route

Routes to the right approver based on your policy — amount band, vendor risk, GL account. The decision happens in Slack, Teams, NetSuite or SAP, wherever your team already works. Sidebar in Gmail shows the live state.

Slack · Teams · NetSuite · SAP · Gmail
4

Post

Once approved, Solden writes the bill to your ERP — correct GL accounts, vendor balance, FX, document references. Treasury runs the payment batch out of the ERP on its own schedule.

No payment trigger

Built for finance, not consumers

Defaults that an audit committee can sign off on.

Auditable by default

Every action — capture, validation, routing, post — lands in an append-only audit trail tied to the record. Tamper-protected via database triggers, not application logic. Your auditors get a single ref ID per AP item.

Deterministic where it matters

Matching, tolerance, sanctions, budget gates and 3-way logic are rules, not models. The model reads unstructured input and writes operator-facing prose — it doesn't get a vote on routing.

Zero email to vendors

Solden never emails your vendors and never authors vendor-facing body text. Vendor outreach stays with the operator. Less surface area, less reputational risk.

Sits beside the ERP

We don't replace NetSuite, SAP, QuickBooks or Xero. Solden runs the cross-checks they don't and writes the bill back when it's approved. Payment execution stays in the ERP.
