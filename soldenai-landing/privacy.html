What we do with your data.

Marketing site. Workspace. Browser extensions. Approval surfaces. What we collect. What we do with it. How long we keep it.

Who we are

Solden Technologies Ltd., a UK company building the workflow runtime for the back office. This page covers everything we do with your data: the marketing site you're reading now, the workspace you sign into, the browser extensions you install, and the approval surfaces we deliver into your chat tools. The specifics of how we process customer-tenant data under contract are spelled out in our DPA, which we sign before any production data flows.

What the marketing site collects

From the contact form: your name, work email, company, role, primary ERP, topic, and a short message about your back office. That is it.

From your visit: standard server logs (IP address, user agent, page, timestamp) and one first-party preference cookie. No third-party analytics. No tracking pixels. No advertising cookies.

What the workspace collects

Workspace identity for everyone the customer adds: email address, name, organization, role. Authentication metadata: last sign-in, OAuth tokens (encrypted at rest), session timestamps. Workflow data: AP records, vendor master records, approval decisions, ERP posting state, the agent's reasoning + governance verdict on every routing decision, and the append-only audit trail.

The workspace at workspace.soldenai.com is the coordination-layer control center. It does not read inboxes directly. That work happens in the browser extensions described below.

What the browser extensions do

Solden ships a Gmail extension and an Outlook add-in that surface a contextual sidebar on invoice threads. When you open an invoice email with one installed, Solden reads the email's content and metadata so the agent can extract bill details, run validation, and route the record for approval through the chat tool the approver lives in.

The Gmail extension uses Google's gmail.modify scope to:

  • Read the subject, sender, headers, body, and attachments on the active invoice thread.
  • Read invoice attachments (PDF, image) and pass them to the extraction pipeline.
  • Apply a Solden label to threads we've processed.
  • Archive threads after the agent posts the bill to your ERP (configurable per workspace).

The Outlook add-in uses Microsoft Graph's Mail.Read and Mail.ReadWrite scopes for the same purpose on Microsoft 365 inboxes.

Neither extension reads inbox contents beyond the threads you open with the sidebar, sends email on your behalf, or accesses your contacts. Neither extension approves or rejects invoices. Those decisions are routed to the chat-tool approval cards described below.

What the approval surfaces do

Solden delivers approval cards into the channel the workspace admin configures on Slack or Microsoft Teams. We read the approval click + any note the approver adds. We do not read channel history, direct messages, or membership beyond verifying the responding user is on the workspace's approver list.

Where it lives

Solden runs on managed hosting in US and EU regions. Each customer organization has its own logical tenant; rows are scoped by organization id and writes are gated by per-table constraints. Customer data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256). Database backups are retained for 30 days with the same encryption. OAuth tokens are encrypted at rest with per-deployment keys held in the runtime environment, not in the database.

Sub-processors

Solden uses a small set of sub-processors to operate the product: a managed-hosting provider, a transactional-email service for system notices, an error-monitoring service (PII scrubbed at the application boundary), and a language-model API for narrative summaries that surface in operator views. Deterministic AP routing (matching, validation, approval gating, ERP posting) does not call any language model.

The current named list, with sub-processor locations and data categories, is shared on request to privacy@soldenai.com and is governed by the customer's DPA. We notify workspace owners of material changes before they take effect.

Third-party services you connect

Separate from our sub-processors, your workspace can connect to systems your organization already runs. These remain your systems; we read or write to them under the scopes you authorize. The connections are:

  • Google Workspace or Microsoft 365, for the Gmail or Outlook intake described above.
  • Slack or Microsoft Teams, for the approval surfaces described above.
  • Your ERP (QuickBooks Online, Xero, NetSuite, SAP, or other supported back-office systems), Solden writes bills, payments, and journal entries you configure; reads master data you authorize.

Disconnect any of them at any time from Settings → Connections. Disconnecting revokes the stored credential immediately and Solden stops accessing the system within the next agent cycle.

How long we keep it

Marketing-site conversation records: while the relationship is active, plus 24 months after the last exchange. Server logs: 90 days.

Tenant data: the lifetime of the customer relationship. The audit chain is retained per the customer's chosen policy (default 7 years, configurable in workspace settings to meet sector requirements). On contract termination we delete tenant data within 30 days unless the customer requests an export window first; legal-hold data is retained per the DPA.

Your rights

You can ask us to show you what we hold on you, correct anything wrong, export it, or delete it. Workspace owners can request export or deletion of the tenant's data. Email privacy@soldenai.com and we will respond within 30 days. If you are in the EU or UK, you also have the right to complain to your local data protection authority.

Data sharing

We do not sell, syndicate, or share customer or end-user data outside the sub-processor set described above. Aggregated, de-identified usage statistics may inform product improvements; no row of personal or tenant data leaves Solden's control for any other purpose.

Changes

If anything material shifts, we update this page and bump the version stamp at the top. Material product changes are notified to workspace owners by email. Last reviewed: 19 May 2026.
