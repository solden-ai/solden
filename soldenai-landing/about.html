About

Finance teams don't pick a tool. They live across them.

Solden is the coordination layer that keeps an AP record coherent as it crosses Gmail, Slack, Teams, NetSuite and SAP. We built it because every finance team we've worked with had the same complaint: the work fragments faster than the ERP can catch.

Why this, why now

ERPs are systems of record. They were never designed to be the place where a controller asks an AP analyst a question, or where an approver gets a Slack ping at 5pm on a Friday. So that work happens elsewhere, in inboxes, threads, spreadsheets, DMs, and the ERP gets a clean entry only at the very end. The middle is invisible.

That gap is where errors live. Duplicate invoices, mis-coded GL, vendor-master drift, unsigned approvals, lost context on disputed POs. The ERP can't show you any of it because the ERP wasn't part of the conversation.

Solden does the cross-checks the ERP doesn't run, and keeps the conversation tied to the record. One AP item, one auditable thread, every surface your team already uses. We don't replace the ERP. We don't author your vendor master. We don't initiate payments. We do the coordination work that humans currently do over Slack at 11pm.

How we build

Deterministic where it matters: matching, tolerance, sanctions, budget, 3-way logic. The model reads invoices and writes prose; it doesn't get a vote on routing. Append-only audit trail enforced at the database, not the application. Least-privilege scopes on every integration. We default to behaviours an audit committee can sign off on, then add ergonomics on top.

Tools are render targets, not data homes. Gmail is for capture and context. Slack and Teams are for decisions. NetSuite and SAP are for the record. Solden writes back to whichever surface your team is in, and the audit trail follows the record, not the surface.
