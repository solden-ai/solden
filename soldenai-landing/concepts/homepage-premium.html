01 / The problem

Every system holds a slice. None holds the work.

ERP

Holds the result

Knows what posted. Not what is stuck, or who owes the next step.

Email

Holds fragments

Fourteen threads, no single truth, no owner attached.

Slack

Holds the decision

The approval happens, then the context evaporates.

Your team

Holds the rest

The workflow lives in someone's head, carried by hand.

02 / First shipped proof

Accounts payable is the first thing it runs.

A 70-person SaaS company

Two-person finance team. 180 invoices a month.

Invoices land in a shared Gmail inbox. The finance lead forwards each one, chases approvers in Slack, then keys the approved ones into NetSuite by hand. Approvals sit for a week. Duplicates slip through. Month-end is reconciliation against memory.

Solden reads every invoice, matches it to its PO, routes the approval to the budget owner in Slack where they already work, posts to NetSuite when it clears policy, and keeps the full trail on the record. Cycle time drops from 17 days to under 5.
